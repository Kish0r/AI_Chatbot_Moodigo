If You're in Crisis - Take These Steps

1
Call for Immediate Help

If you're having thoughts of suicide or self-harm, call 988 or 911 immediately. Don't wait - help is available right now.

2
Reach Out to Someone

Contact a trusted friend, family member, counselor, or mentor. You don't have to go through this alone.

3
Stay Safe

Remove any means of self-harm from your immediate environment. Stay with someone or go to a safe place like an emergency room.

4
Follow Up

Connect with ongoing mental health support. Consider counseling, therapy, or support groups for long-term wellness.

Warning Signs to Watch For

Immediate Warning Signs:
  • Talking about wanting to die or kill oneself
  • Looking for ways to kill oneself
  • Talking about feeling hopeless or having no purpose
  • Talking about feeling trapped or in unbearable pain
  • Talking about being a burden to others
Additional Warning Signs:
  • Increasing use of alcohol or drugs
  • Acting anxious, agitated, or reckless
  • Sleeping too little or too much
  • Withdrawing or feeling isolated
  • Showing rage or talking about seeking revenge

If you notice these signs in yourself or someone else, take them seriously and seek help immediately.
